# Three-minute demo script

## V1.2 operator console flow

1. Open **Demo controls** and select **Seed all companies**.
2. Open `/companies` (the operator portfolio) and compare AtlasAI, FinPilot, HealthSync, DevToolsHub, and GreenLedger — note the **Score**, **Status**, **Top risk**, and **Review status** columns. Every analyzed company starts as a `needs_review` draft.
3. Open AtlasAI to show the strict 53.4 score and 78–84 recovery path. Point out the **Draft analysis — requires operator review** banner.
4. Return to the portfolio and select **Intake company**.
5. Create a company manually, then open its evidence-intake workspace.
6. Add one financial row and one compliance item; optionally paste an investor note.
7. Select **Run analysis** and show the partial, source-backed dashboard.
8. Show the generated dashboard and export the company's Markdown diligence report.
9. Emphasize that every generated output — score, concerns, preparation notes, cleanup queue, and report — is a **draft for operator review**, not legal, tax, investment, or accounting advice.

The point of the V1.2 demo is that the product is no longer a fixed AtlasAI story and is not positioned as a self-serve replacement for an embedded team: the same local workflow persists and analyzes arbitrary founder-provided evidence into auditable operator-review drafts.

## 0:00–0:25 — Introduce the problem

“Fundraise readiness is usually scattered across a data room, finance spreadsheet, cap table, HR records, and the founder’s memory of investor meetings. Flowlie turns that fragmented back office into one diligence preparation workflow.”

Open the landing page and select **Run the AtlasAI demo**.

## 0:25–0:45 — Show the demo company

“AtlasAI is a synthetic Seed-stage AI sales automation company raising $3 million. It has $420K ARR, $680K cash, a $95K monthly burn, employees and contractors, an active enterprise pipeline, and several intentional diligence gaps.”

Select **Seed & analyze AtlasAI**.

## 0:45–1:15 — Run the analysis

“This one local action loads the synthetic evidence and runs deterministic scoring, risk rules, template Q&A, and action planning. There is no paid API and no external company data.”

Open the dashboard.

## 1:15–1:45 — Strict score and recovery path

“The dashboard starts with a Strict Raise Readiness Score of 53.4, which puts AtlasAI in the Not diligence-ready tier. The product does not inflate that result. Instead, the Recovery Path shows how completing specific evidence work removes 25.6 weighted penalty points and projects a 78–84 outcome.”

Point out the before-versus-after card, then open **Financials** briefly. “AtlasAI has 7.1 months of runway. Revenue grew, but burn rose from $59K to $95K and gross margin fell from 72% to 54%.”

## 1:40–2:05 — Risk flags and data room

Open **Risk flags**.

“The engine identifies potential diligence concerns and shows the evidence, why each issue matters to investors, the business impact, and a suggested fix. The highest-priority issues are runway, unsigned contractor IP, and customer concentration.”

Open **Data room** to show present versus missing items.

## 2:05–2:30 — Investor Q&A

Open **Investor Q&A**.

“Flowlie converts the same evidence into the questions investors are likely to ask. Every draft answer names its sources and separately states the missing evidence, so the system cannot quietly invent readiness.”

Expand the runway, margin, or contractor-IP answer.

## 2:30–2:50 — Seven-day plan

Open **Action plan**.

“Analysis becomes an owned seven-day sprint. Each task shows either an estimated strict-score lift or ‘preparedness only,’ so the founder can distinguish formal readiness work from better storytelling.”

## 2:50–3:00 — Why it matters

“The value is not another static checklist. It is a local, evidence-backed preparation layer that helps a founder know what investors may ask, what they can answer today, and what the team should fix next. The full work product can also be exported as a Markdown diligence report.”
